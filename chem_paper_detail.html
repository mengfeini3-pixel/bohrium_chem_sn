摘要

Background: Gestational hypertension and preeclampsia are significant complications of pregnancy affecting approximately 5-10% of pregnancies worldwide. Low-dose aspirin has been recommended for prevention in high-risk women. MicroRNA-210-3p has been implicated in hypoxia and vascular dysfunction. This prospective pilot study aimed to investigate the longitudinal trajectory of circulating microRNA-210-3p and its association with aspirin use in these conditions.

Methods: A prospective pilot study was conducted involving 120 pregnant women at high risk for gestational hypertension/preeclampsia. Participants were divided into aspirin-treated (n=60) and control (n=60) groups. Blood samples were collected at three time points (12-16 weeks, 20-24 weeks, and 28-32 weeks of gestation). Circulating microRNA-210-3p levels were measured using quantitative real-time PCR. Clinical outcomes including blood pressure, proteinuria, and pregnancy complications were recorded.

Results: Circulating microRNA-210-3p levels significantly increased from 12-16 weeks to 28-32 weeks in the control group but remained stable in the aspirin-treated group. Women who developed preeclampsia showed significantly higher microRNA-210-3p levels at 20-24 weeks compared to those who remained normotensive (fold change 2.8, p < 0.001). Aspirin use was associated with attenuated elevation of microRNA-210-3p (p < 0.01). ROC analysis demonstrated moderate predictive value of microRNA-210-3p for preeclampsia (AUC = 0.75).
